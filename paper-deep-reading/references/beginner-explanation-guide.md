# Beginner Explanation Guide

## Audience

Assume the reader is smart, motivated, and new to the field. Explain hidden prerequisites without flattening the science.

## When To Add Explanations

Add a beginner callout when the paper first introduces:

- A domain concept.
- A method or instrument mode.
- A material structure or phase.
- A graph axis or unit.
- A mechanism step.
- A theoretical term.
- A claim that depends on multiple earlier facts.

## Explanation Pattern

Use this order:

1. Plain-language definition.
2. Why it matters in this paper.
3. What to look for in the figure or data.
4. One boundary or common confusion.

## Claim Boundary Language

Keep evidence categories clear:

- "The authors measured..."
- "The authors calculated..."
- "The authors inferred..."
- "The result is consistent with..."
- "This does not by itself prove..."

## Avoid

- Decorative analogies that do not return to the data.
- Explaining every basic word until the note becomes noisy.
- Turning uncertainty into certainty.
- Repeating the same concept in full after it already appears in the question log or glossary.
